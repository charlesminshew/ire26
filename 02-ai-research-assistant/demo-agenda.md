# Demo Agenda — Your AI Research Assistant (Live Demo)

Format: I demo each task once, then you try it with a sample file. Every sample is pre-staged at **bit.ly/ire-ai-2026** so nobody hunts for data. Prompts are in [demo-tasks.md](demo-tasks.md) — copy, don't retype.

> **The one rule:** AI is great at *transforming* (reshaping, extracting, comparing) and risky on *facts*. Spot-check everything it pulls against the source before you trust it.

## Which are true hands-on vs. watch-along

- **Hands-on (everyone runs it):** Tasks 1, 2, 4 — fast, reliable, satisfying in under 10 minutes.
- **Watch-along (follow my screen, replay later):** Tasks 3 and 5 take longer and are easy to redo at home with the saved prompts.

This keeps energy up: three quick wins people complete in the room, two bigger "wow" moments they watch and take home.

## Sample files to pre-stage (in the bit.ly folder)

1. **A listing web page** — saved HTML or a live URL with ~30 rows (e.g. inspection results, a roster, a permit list).
2. **A messy PDF** — a real-looking report with a table that isn't copy-pasteable (campaign finance, an inspection report).
3. **Two versions of a document** — a short bill or contract with 3–4 substantive changes, one of them buried.
4. **A messy name/address column** — ~40 rows with inconsistent capitalization, abbreviations, and a couple of near-duplicates that should *not* be merged.
5. **A JSON file of records** — 50–100 rows for the browse-app demo (can be the cleaned output of Task 2 or 4).

> Tip: make Task 2's output feed Task 5's input. Seeing one task's result become the next task's raw material is the most "aha" moment of the hour.

## If you're short on time

Cut Task 3 first (it's the slowest to land), then Task 5's hands-on portion (demo only). Tasks 1, 2, and 4 are the core: extract, structure, clean.

## Backup plan

Live demos break. Have the *expected output* of each task saved in the bit.ly folder so if a tool stalls or the wifi dies, you can show the finished result and keep moving.
